# Visualizing Gene Structure and Variants Using UCSC Genome Browser

## Project Overview
This project demonstrates how to explore gene structure and genetic variants using the UCSC Genome Browser. The aim is to visualize exons, introns, regulatory regions, and genetic variants in a selected human gene and understand their biological significance.

The analysis focuses on identifying important genomic features such as transcription start sites, CpG islands, and clinically relevant variants.

---

## Objective

- To visualize gene structure in the human genome
- To identify exons and introns in a selected gene
- To explore regulatory regions such as promoters and CpG islands
- To identify genetic variants associated with diseases
- To compare gene conservation across species

---

## Gene Selected

Gene Name: **TP53**

TP53 is a tumor suppressor gene that plays a critical role in controlling cell division and preventing cancer formation. Mutations in this gene are commonly associated with various cancers.

---

## Tools Used

- UCSC Genome Browser
- NCBI Gene Database
- ClinVar Variant Database
- 1000 Genomes Project Data

---

## Steps Performed

### Step 1: Gene Identification

The TP53 gene was searched in the UCSC Genome Browser using the human genome assembly (hg38). The genomic location of the gene was identified.

### Step 2: Visualization of Gene Structure

The gene structure was examined using the RefSeq gene annotation track. This allowed identification of:

- Exons (coding regions)
- Introns (non-coding regions between exons)

### Step 3: Identification of Regulatory Regions

Regulatory features around the gene were analyzed using:

- CpG Island track
- Transcription Start Site (TSS) information
- Promoter-associated regions

These elements help regulate gene expression.

### Step 4: Variant Analysis

ClinVar and 1000 Genomes tracks were used to identify genetic variants present within the TP53 gene region.

Variants were categorized as:

- Benign
- Likely benign
- Pathogenic
- Likely pathogenic

Pathogenic variants are associated with disease conditions.

### Step 5: Comparative Genomics

The conservation track was used to compare the gene region across species such as:

- Human
- Mouse
- Rat

Highly conserved regions indicate important biological functions.

## Key Findings

1. The TP53 gene contains multiple exons and introns.
2. CpG islands are present near the promoter region, suggesting regulation of gene expression.
3. Several variants from the ClinVar database were identified in the gene region.
4. Some variants are associated with cancer-related diseases.
5. Conservation analysis shows that the gene is highly conserved across species.

---

## Conclusion

The UCSC Genome Browser provides an effective platform for visualizing gene structure and genomic variations. Analysis of the TP53 gene revealed important structural and regulatory features including exons, introns, CpG islands, and disease-associated variants.

Comparative genomics analysis further showed that the gene is evolutionarily conserved, highlighting its essential role in cellular regulation and cancer prevention.
